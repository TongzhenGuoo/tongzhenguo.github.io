About Me

QUESTION

ANSWER

01
What kind of designer are you?

I am a graphic designer who values structure and logic.

I focus on how visual systems create order, and how design can strengthen communication through clarity and structure. For me, every project should go beyond surface aesthetics and be grounded in clear concepts and contextual research.

Design is not decoration; it is a way of organizing information and constructing meaning.

02
How do you usually begin a project?

I begin by understanding the context and objectives.

I analyze the background, audience, and core problem before defining a clear visual direction. Only after establishing a solid logical framework do I move into developing the visual language.

I believe a stable and coherent visual system is more important than a single striking image.

03
What do you value most in design?

Clarity, consistency, and sustainability.

I aim to create work that remains coherent across different applications and has the flexibility to evolve. Whether it is a brand system, typographic structure, or information layout, I consider its long-term potential rather than designing for a single output.

04
What are your strengths?
  • Structured thinking
  • Concept-driven visual expression
  • Sensitivity to typographic detail
  • Ability to maintain consistency within visual systems

    • Clarity, consistency, and sustainability.

    05
    How do you view collaboration?

    I see design as a process of communication and collaboration.

    In teamwork, I value clarity and timely feedback. I prefer to move forward collectively based on a shared understanding of the project goals, rather than working in isolation.

    Good communication improves both the quality and efficiency of a project.

    06
    What is your design philosophy?

    Design is a structured way of thinking.

    It helps us organize complex information, define priorities, and communicate essential meaning through visual systems.

    I strive to create work that is visually engaging while remaining logically coherent.
